‚ùÑÔ∏è Safety & Health (Winter Essentials)

‚úÖ DO‚ùå DON'T
Wear Layers: Strictly follow the 3-layer system (Thermals + Fleece/Sweater + Waterproof Jacket) to regulate body temperature.Wear Cotton/Denim in Snow: These materials absorb moisture, stay wet, and will make you extremely cold, risking sickness.
Hydrate Constantly: Drink warm water, tea, or Thukpa frequently. High altitude and cold air cause rapid dehydration.Consume Alcohol Heavily: Alcohol can increase the risk of hypothermia and dehydration, and it impairs judgment on icy paths.
Carry Cash: While the main Mall Road has ATMs/digital pay, rural Sethan, Sissu, and small dhabas may require cash.Trust Only Hotel Blankets: Always check if your hotel provides a good room heater and an electric blanket. Cold nights are brutal!
Negotiate Taxi Fares: Bargain with local taxi drivers, especially for full-day bookings like Sissu and Sethan, as you are a group of six.Self-Drive Recklessly: Unless you are a seasoned mountain driver with a 4x4 and snow chains, hire a local, experienced driver.
Use Sun Protection: Wear UV-blocking sunglasses, sunscreen, and SPF lip balm. Sun reflection off the snow is intense.Go Out on Icy Paths Alone at Night: Icy spots are common, especially on side roads. Stick to well-lit areas, particularly in Old Manali.

üèõÔ∏è Culture & Environment

‚úÖ DO‚ùå DON'T
Respect Religious Sites: When visiting temples (like Hadimba or Vashisht), dress modestly (full coverage) and always remove your shoes. Cover your head when entering a Gurudwara or Dargah.Litter or Use Polythene: Himachal Pradesh is very strict about cleanliness. Carry all wrappers, plastic, and waste back to the nearest dustbin. Polythene is banned.
Try Local Food: Seek out local eateries for authentic Himachali dishes like Siddu and fresh Trout Fish.Disregard Local Advice: If a local, the hotel staff, or your cab driver advises against a route (e.g., due to ice or a permit issue), follow their guidance.
Support Local Businesses: Buy souvenirs, woolen clothes, and handicrafts from local markets (Tibetan Market, Mall Road).Deface Property: Do not scribble or carve on rocks, trees, or buildings.
Be Prepared for Permits: Know that permits are required for vehicles to certain high-altitude zones (like Sethan in winter, or Rohtang Pass, which is closed).Take Photos Without Consent: Always ask permission before photographing local people, especially women or monks in monasteries.

üöò Tourist Traps & Convenience

‚úÖ DO‚ùå DON'T
Check Hotel Amenities: Confirm your hotel has 24-hour running hot water and reliable power backup (generator), as power cuts are common in winter.Feel Obligated to Rent Snow Gear: If you have your own high-quality, waterproof jacket and boots, you usually don't need to rent the local 'snow dress' unless your gear is insufficient.
Stick to Reputable Tour Operators: For adventure sports like skiing in Solang, ensure the operator is certified and uses proper safety equipment.Get Convinced by Strangers: Avoid touts or random people at the bus stand/Mall Road who try to steer you toward specific hotels, restaurants, or taxi services for commission.
Keep Offline Maps Ready: Mobile network (especially 4G) can be unreliable in Sethan and Sissu. Download Google Maps for the area beforehand.Overtake on Turns: Mountain roads require patience. Never try to overtake vehicles on blind corners. Safety first for all six of us!
